What to Expect During a Laser Hair Removal Session

One of the most common concerns we hear is about the treatment experience itself. Many people wonder: Does it hurt? How long does it take? Will I need downtime afterward?

We’re here to answer all those questions and more.

Each laser hair removal session typically lasts between 15 minutes to an hour, depending on the area being treated. Smaller areas like the underarms or upper lip can be completed quickly, while larger areas such as the legs or back may take longer.

Before the procedure, we apply a cooling gel to protect the skin and reduce any discomfort. Most of our clients compare the sensation to a light snap from a rubber band—noticeable, but manageable. And because our equipment includes cooling technology, it helps soothe the skin during and after the laser pulses.

After your session, you may experience slight redness or sensitivity, similar to a mild sunburn. These effects usually disappear within a few hours. There’s no required downtime, which means you can return to work or run errands immediately after your appointment.

How Many Sessions Will You Need?

Laser hair removal is not a one-time treatment. Hair grows in cycles, and the laser is most effective during the active growth phase. That’s why we recommend a series of six to eight treatments, spaced several weeks apart, to catch all the follicles at the right time.

Depending on your hair type, skin tone, and the area being treated, you might need occasional maintenance sessions after your initial series. We’ll keep track of your progress and let you know if any touch-ups are necessary.

Many of our clients are thrilled with the results even after just a few visits. Areas that were once prone to stubble and irritation become smoother and easier to manage. It’s an investment in your daily comfort, confidence, and routine.

Preparing for Your First Appointment

If you’re ready to get started, there are a few simple steps to prepare for your first visit:

  1. Shave the treatment area 24 hours before your appointment. This helps the laser target the hair follicle directly.

  2. Avoid sun exposure for at least two weeks prior to treatment. Sunburned or tanned skin can affect the safety of the procedure.

  3. Skip waxing or plucking for four to six weeks before your session. The laser needs the hair follicle to be intact.

  4. Tell us about any medications you’re taking. Some medications can increase sensitivity to light, so we’ll adjust accordingly.

We’ll walk you through all of this during your consultation and send reminders before each session.
